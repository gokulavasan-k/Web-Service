Japan


Location
Japan is an island nation in East Asia, surrounded by the Pacific Ocean, the Sea of Japan, the Sea of Okhotsk, and the East China Sea.
Geography
Japan is made up of 6,852 islands, including the four main islands of Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu. The country is mountainous, with forests covering two-thirds of its land.
Climate
Japan has a temperate climate with four seasons, except for Okinawa in the south, which is subtropical, and Hokkaido in the north, which is subarctic.
Population
Japan is one of the world's most densely populated countries, with 127 million residents. Most people live on the main islands of Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku.
Government
Japan is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary government. The emperor is the head of state, and the prime minister is the head of government.
Economy
Japan has a market economy and is a member of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) and the Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP).
History
The first recorded history of Japan dates back to the migration of the Yayoi people from mainland Asia between 300 BCE and 300 CE.
Rivers
Japan's rivers are generally short and swift-running, and there are few natural water reservoirs.
Tidal flats
Japan has 765 square kilometers of tidal flats, ranking it 35th in the world for tidal flat extent.
